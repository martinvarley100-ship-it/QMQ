The Loyalty Tax model

How the same brief is priced for three different actors. Built on 200,000+ AIM orders.

The thesis. A loyal customer pays more to their incumbent supplier than a new customer would. The difference is real value, paid for switching cost, trust, and predictable performance. The Loyalty Tax model quantifies that premium, helping incumbents defend it and helping challengers know exactly what they have to beat.

The formula

Simplified for illustration
Defensible bid = Network typical + Loyalty Tax
where
Loyalty Tax = Network typical × (R/10 × 0.04 + F/10 × 0.02 + M/10 × 0.01)
R, F, M each on a 0 to 10 scale.
Max Loyalty Tax = 7% of network typical at R=F=M=10.
Worked example. Brightline at R9 F9 M8 against BruMate. Loyalty Tax = $5.40 × (9/10 × 0.04 + 9/10 × 0.02 + 8/10 × 0.01) = $5.40 × 0.062 = $0.33. Round to $0.34. Defensible bid = $5.40 + $0.34 = $5.74. Below network typical Low band of $5.40, BruMate is leaving money on the table.

If you are the incumbent

The Loyalty Tax tells you what your relationship is worth in pricing terms. On Pro tier we surface it on every bid where R, F, and M sum to 18 or higher. Below your defensible bid, you are giving away margin. Above by more than 10%, you risk the challenger winning.

If you are the challenger

The model shows you the threshold without naming the incumbent. You see "the incumbent has a $0.34 advantage built in," not who they are. To win, your landed price needs to clear that threshold, not necessarily by much. Often the freight differential is what closes the gap.
